The problem

Compute is the bottleneck — and power, not chips, is the constraint.

Inference is overtaking training as the dominant AI workload: steady, 24/7, latency-sensitive, exploding. Meanwhile centralized supply can't keep up — grid interconnection runs 5–7 years and new data centers are multi-year, multi-billion-dollar builds.

The opening

The power is already in the building.

A service-upgraded home — a 400A panel, a GPU node, and owned ~20+ kW solar and battery — carries the heavy electrical service dense compute needs, with power-ready commercial and light-industrial space as a secondary archetype. So we stand up GPU capacity next to the teams that need it. As inference moves closer to where it's used, the thesis keeps validating.

The model — two phases
Now · Phase 1

Managed brokerage

Source GPU capacity on the open market, run it as a managed service, and resell to inference teams at open-market rates that undercut the hyperscalers. Asset-light, revenue now — and it proves demand and signs the offtake that de-risks Phase 2.

Next · Phase 2

Owned regional capacity

Deploy owned GPU clusters, with battery backup to keep them online through outages. Priority sites are service-upgraded homes: a 400A panel, a GPU node, and an owned 20+ kW solar-plus-battery array, so the §48E clean-energy credit (30%+) offsets the node's biggest cost, power. Power-ready commercial and light-industrial space is the secondary archetype. We own the hardware and the customer relationship, and hardware is financed against signed offtake.

Why it works

The moat is deployment.

A sales-and-deployment engine that puts owned compute where it's needed — and owns the capacity and the customer relationship. Compute will commoditize; being the one who can stand it up fast, close to demand, won't. A proven recurring-revenue sales-and-install engine, pointed at AI's biggest constraint.

Economics & upside

Strong unit economics, financed against contracts.

Base case per node (a flagship 8×H100 server, ~$250K): ~$9.8K/mo gross, ~$7.6K/mo net, payback ~year 3, ~+$170K net over 5 years. Scaling to 1,000+ nodes across Austin, Dallas, Boston and California drives the network into nine figures of annual net. (Illustrative; requires contracted offtake.)

We match the GPU to the workload — power-efficient L40S nodes for the broad inference market, H100/H200 for the most demanding models. This puts more revenue-generating compute per kilowatt on power-constrained sites.
