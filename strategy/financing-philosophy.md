# Financing philosophy — minimize dilution, debt-finance the buildout, keep control

**Captured:** 2026-06-30 · Not legal/tax/financial advice — confirm structure with the Atlas startup attorney + CPA.

## The north star
> **Minimize and control dilution — don't chase zero.** Raise as little equity as possible, fund the
> capital-heavy buildout with debt, and make sure every round leaves our stake worth *more*.

## The hard truth (why "zero dilution while raising equity" isn't possible)
Selling equity *is* dilution — if an investor buys 15% of the company, that 15% comes proportionally
out of every existing owner. There is no equity round with zero dilution. What we protect instead:

- **Percentage ≠ value.** 60% of a $2M company is $1.2M; 51% of a $50M company is $25.5M. Smaller
  slice, bigger pie. That's *healthy* dilution.
- **Percentage ≠ control.** You can drop below 50% and still control the company.

## The actual tools to "not dilute" (non-dilutive capital)
- **Debt / venture debt** — borrow, repay, give up **zero** ownership. The **owned-node buildout is
  debt-financed against signed contracts** — non-dilutive by design.
- **Revenue** — Phase-1 brokerage cash reinvested = bootstrapped, no dilution.
- **Tax credits & grants** — ITC / depreciation stack = free capital.
- **Customer prepayments** — committed contracts fund the gear they run on.

## The tradeoff (be honest)
Debt must be repaid even if things go sideways, and lenders want revenue/collateral we don't have
pre-launch. **Equity is patient risk capital** — it funds the scary early phase debt won't. So a
*small* early equity raise (the SAFE) is worth the dilution; the capital-heavy buildout is debt.

## Protect control, not percentage
1. Keep founder **majority** as long as possible.
2. **Control the board** — board seats matter more than ownership % for control.
3. Consider **dual-class shares** later — founders hold super-voting stock, keeping voting control
   even when economically diluted (how Zuckerberg / Google founders did it). Seed investors sometimes
   resist early, so usually a later-stage tool.

## What this means for the partners
We **cannot** promise partners a *frozen percentage* — when we raise, everyone dilutes together,
including the founder. We promise a **fixed number of shares (their grant)**; we all rise and fall
together as the company grows. **Vesting** (4-yr, 1-yr cliff) still protects us if someone leaves.

## Must-dos before granting any equity (founder protection)
- **Vesting** on every grant (yours too): 4-year, 1-year cliff. Non-negotiable.
- **83(b) election within 30 days** of each grant.
- **LLC vs. individual** — granting to an LLC can complicate **QSBS** and 83(b); confirm per person
  with the CPA before issuing.
- **Contingent, written offers** — restricted-stock purchase agreements (Atlas/Clerky handle these).
- **Option pool** — investors often want ~10% ESOP, which dilutes founders again; budget for it now.
- **Equity = contribution, not loyalty** — co-founder-level grants (~11%) only for full-time builders;
  part-time/help = advisor grants (0.25–1%) or options.
