Where the compute demand is — and where we cluster nodes.

Red intensity = concentration of AI / inference & cloud demand (where customers' apps and data-center ecosystems live). We place node clusters on the hot spots for latency & data-residency-sensitive workloads, and route batch workloads to cheap-power / high-sun zones.
Tier 1 — flagship cluster (top demand) Tier 2 — major cluster Tier 3 — edge node Sun / cheap-power build zone (batch)
The vision: every cluster is one node in a single virtual data center — distributed across the country's demand centers, self-powered by on-site solar + battery, and stitched into one managed fabric. No single campus to permit, power, or cool; we scale by adding nodes where demand and cheap energy are — aiming, in aggregate, to exceed any single hyperscaler or xAI campus.
Latency & compliance-sensitive workloads → run on the red demand clusters (near customers' apps + U.S. data residency). Batch / async workloads → routed to the green cheap-power, high-sun zones (Southwest first; Hawaii / Puerto Rico as self-power showcases).
Heatmap is illustrative — based on concentration of data-center, cloud-region, and AI-company activity, not exact demand figures. Heliode, Inc. — confidential.
