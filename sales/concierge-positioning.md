# Positioning — the Concierge differentiator

**Captured:** 2026-06-28 · The thing that sets Heliode apart from AWS and every other GPU cloud.

## The core promise
> **A real person, not a ticket queue.** Anytime a customer needs something — a change, a question,
> a problem — they reach a named human who answers fast, handles it for them, and is always working
> to cut their bill. Cheaper *and* personal.

## Why it's a real wedge
The hyperscalers (AWS, GCP, Azure) are faceless: a console, a ticket queue, a chatbot, an account
rep you can't reach. For a small/mid AI team that lives or dies on uptime and cost, **white-glove,
responsive, human service is genuinely scarce** — and it's something a focused operator can deliver
far better than a trillion-dollar platform. It pairs perfectly with the price wedge: we're not just
cheaper, we're cheaper *with a human who keeps making it cheaper.*

## The two-part value proposition (lead with both)
1. **We cut your bottom line.** Same H100/H200 workload, a fraction of hyperscaler on-demand rates —
   and your named contact keeps hunting for savings, not just at signup.
2. **A real person, always.** No ticket queue, no chatbot. Fast responses, changes handled for you,
   a human who knows your setup.

## Language that works
- "A real person, not a ticket queue."
- "The human layer AWS doesn't have."
- "Cheaper, and personal."
- "We don't just lower your bill once — we keep lowering it."
- "Need a change? You text a person, not a portal."

## How to keep it TRUE (don't overpromise)
Say **"answers fast / a named contact / always working to cut your bill"** — NOT a literal
"24/7 instant anytime." As a solo/small team, a same-business-day, named-human SLA you actually hit
beats a "24/7" claim you can't. Sophisticated buyers trust the specific, sustainable promise more.
As you scale, you can formalize it (e.g., a stated response-time SLA per tier).
