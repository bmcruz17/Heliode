Focus

Operator playbook

Everything you need on the phone and in the inbox. Two engines run in parallel: supply (cheap GPU capacity to resell) and demand (clients to sell it to). The brokerage makes money now; the owned hardware is Phase 2.

🎯 Goal: onboard 2 clients this week

Close motion (repeat per client): get their bill → quote that beats it → agree price + onboarding fee (credited on 6–12mo) → point their endpoint at your sourced capacity → live.

Gate: stand up a RunPod/Lambda account so you can actually deliver this week. Don't wait on reseller deals.

Work warm first — your network closes in days; cold rarely does. Rough math: ~8–12 conversations → ~3–4 quotes → ~2 closes. Lever = volume of bills requested.

🚀 30-day push — funding · office · operations

The unlock: paying clients are your best fundraising asset. Traction (revenue + signed offtake) is what closes the round — so the brokerage push and the raise reinforce each other. Run both at once.

  • Week 1 — operate: supply accounts live; first 2 clients; finalize raise materials (teaser/opportunity/proposal); build investor list (warm + angels); open team conversations.
  • Week 2 — traction + pipeline: first clients live (revenue); start investor pitches; verbal offers to co-founder / first hires; scout office + a power-ready pilot site.
  • Week 3 — commitments: more clients; investor meetings → soft commits; LOI on office/site; finalize SAFE terms with a lawyer.
  • Week 4 — close: first checks / strong commits in; sign office; team onboarded; operations running.

Office (cheap team space) ≠ pilot site (needs high-amp power). Can be the same building if it has the service.

Phase 2 · owned hardware

Talking to hardware vendors

Dell · Supermicro · Thinkmate · Exxact · CDW/SHI

Opener (phone or email) "Hi — I'm sourcing 8-GPU HGX H100 servers for an AI inference deployment. HGX-class, 8× H100 SXM5 80GB, similar to a Dell XE9680, but I want to compare integrators. We're an operator standing up regional GPU capacity — looking at ~50 units, phased, more to follow. I'd like a quote on a flagship H100 build and a cost-efficient L40S build."

Terms to drop (so you speak the language)

HGX H100 (8-GPU baseboard) — the standard 8-GPU platform = the "node."
SXM5 vs PCIe — SXM = GPUs on a high-bandwidth board with NVLink between them (best for big models); PCIe = cards in slots (cheaper). Say "SXM5 unless PCIe pencils out for our model sizes."
NVLink / NVSwitch — the fast interconnect tying the 8 GPUs together.
Inference, not training — "steady 24/7, latency-sensitive."
Independent nodes, no fabric — "independent inference nodes, so no InfiniBand fabric needed — dual 100GbE per node is fine." (signals expertise + saves money)
L40S / A100 — cost-efficient inference alternatives to H100.

What to ask

  1. All-in price per node + the per-GPU portion.
  2. Lead time / allocation — weeks to ship? Are H100s allocation-constrained?
  3. SXM5 vs PCIe — price + what they'd recommend for inference.
  4. Quote a cost-efficient build too — 8× L40S (and/or A100).
  5. Volume pricing at 1 / 5 / 10 / 50 units.
  6. Power: max draw/node, 208/240V three-phase? PSU redundancy.
  7. Rack U height + weight, airflow/cooling.
  8. Warranty + support — next-business-day onsite?
  9. Burn-in / rack integration / drop-ship to a site?
  10. Financing / lease-to-own.
  11. Written quote by email + their direct contact.

What they'll ask you

  • Workload? "LLM inference, mixed model sizes, steady production traffic."
  • Single node or cluster? "Independent inference nodes — no training fabric."
  • Power-ready site? "Leased commercial/light-industrial, high-amp 3-phase; confirming sites now."
  • Timeline? "Pilot ~50 nodes, phased; gathering quotes to lock the build."
  • Reselling? "No — we own and operate, and sell managed compute."

Don't commit on the call: "Send the written quote and I'll review with the team."

Phase 1 · revenue now

Wholesale GPU supply

Get cheap committed capacity to resell managed. The spread = the business.

Outreach email "Hi {name} — we run a managed GPU service for AI-inference teams and are aggregating demand across multiple customers. We want to place committed capacity with a few strong providers and resell it managed. Could you share your reserved/committed $/GPU-hr for H100, A100, and L40S; minimum commitment; whether you have a partner/reseller program; and how fast we can scale up/down? Looking to start now."

Who to contact

  • Neoclouds (committed; ask reseller terms): Lambda, CoreWeave, Crusoe, Together AI, Fluidstack, Nebius, Voltage Park, Hyperstack/NexGen
  • Marketplace/spot (cheap burst): RunPod, Vast.ai, TensorDock
  • Start with 3–4 neoclouds + RunPod/Vast for burst.

What to lock from each

  • $/GPU-hr committed vs on-demand (H100, A100, L40S)
  • Minimum commitment (term + quantity)
  • Reseller / partner program?
  • Scale up/down terms + overage pricing
  • Uptime/SLA, regions, spot pre-emption rules
  • How fast capacity can be live for a customer

Margin check: sell price must beat the client's bill and clear source cost + ops + buffer + margin. Easy win: clients on hyperscaler on-demand (~$8–13/GPU-hr). Thin margin: clients already on raw marketplace (~$2–3) — qualify out.

Phase 1 · demand

Client outreach

Buyer = technical founder / ML / infra lead with a steady inference bill that hurts. Lead with their number; make the ask tiny (just the bill).

Warm (your network — fastest close) "Hey {name} — quick one. You (or someone you know) running AI in production? I'm doing managed GPU compute that usually comes in well under AWS/GCP. Send me a recent GPU bill and I'll tell you exactly what you'd save — no pitch."
Cold "Hi {first} — saw {company} is shipping {product}, so you're running steady inference. Most teams your size pay 2–5× the open-market rate for it. Send me last month's GPU bill and I'll send back what it'd cost on us. No call needed. — {you}, Heliode"
Community reply (help first) "Biggest levers: leave hyperscaler on-demand (marketplace/neocloud is 50–80% cheaper), right-size the GPU, batch with vLLM, quantize. (Disclosure: I run Heliode — we do the first one as a managed service — but the rest save you money regardless.)"

The habit that matters: reply to every inbound within an hour with a real "$X → $Y" number.

Internal · not on the site

Pricing — onboarding fee

  • Amount: $500–$3,000 per client — by account size / integration complexity.
  • When: introduce at contract, never at quote (quote stays free).
  • Credit-back: fully credited against a 6–12 month commitment — rewards commitment, reads like a discount, still filters tire-kickers.
  • Early cohort: optionally waive for the first few to build case studies.
How to phrase it (at contract) "Setup is a one-time $[amount] onboarding fee — that covers us doing the full migration so your team doesn't. Commit to a 6–12 month term and we credit it back in full, so onboarding is effectively free."
Build the team

Recruiting & equity

Hire for the gaps you can't cover. Equity from day one; salaries kick in after the raise. Find the talent first, then shape the role around their superpower.

Roles to fill (in order)

  • 1. Technical co-founder / infra & compute lead — owns the orchestration + billing software that makes many nodes act like one cloud, plus deploy/ops. The single most important hire.
  • 2. Founding engineer (full-stack) — product, customer dashboard, billing, monitoring. Can overlap with #1 early.
  • 3. GTM / sales — you lead; add reps for outbound as volume grows.
  • 4. Ops / deployment lead (Phase 2) — site sourcing, electrical, install, vendor management.
  • Advisors — one with GPU/cloud-infra depth, one who's raised before.

Talent → role (run on each person)

  • What's their superpower — the thing they're clearly great at?
  • What energizes them vs. drains them?
  • Which of your gaps do they fill?
  • Would you work with them daily, for years?
  • The one role where they move the company most — shape it around that.

Equity (directional — confirm with a startup lawyer)

  • Co-founder: meaningful — high single digits to tens of %, by contribution + timing.
  • First ~5 employees: ~0.5–2% each, by role/seniority.
  • Advisors: ~0.1–1%.
  • Vesting: standard 4 years, 1-year cliff — protects everyone if someone leaves early.
  • Create an option pool (~10–15%) for early hires.
  • Grants are securities — use a lawyer; clean cap table + 83(b) elections from day one.
Vision pitch (recruiting) "AI is exploding and the bottleneck isn't chips — it's power and the ability to deploy compute fast. Heliode sells cheaper managed GPU compute today, and we're building owned regional capacity in commercial space — standing up in days, not the years a data center takes. I'm building the founding team now: equity from day one, salaries once we close the raise. I think you'd be great at {role} — want to hear the full picture?"
