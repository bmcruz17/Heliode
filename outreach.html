4Paid ads — Meta — queued · target: AI inference teams

Target: the AI inference teams who buy compute.

Read this first — set expectations. Meta (Facebook/Instagram) can't target "ML engineer" or "AI startup founder" by job title the way LinkedIn can. For a niche technical buyer it's the harder, less efficient channel. Run it anyway, but lean on the three things Meta is actually good at — retargeting, lookalikes, and letting the algorithm optimize on a conversion event — and pair it with direct LinkedIn/X prospecting (Section 3), which will convert this audience better dollar-for-dollar.

How to actually reach buyers on Meta

Targeting
Three audiences, in priority order — start at the top.
1 · Retargeting (highest intent). Install the Meta Pixel on heliode.ai. Show ads to people who visited the site / used the savings calculator but didn't request a quote. This is where Meta earns its keep. 2 · Lookalike audiences. Upload your lead + customer list (even 50–100 emails) → build a 1% Lookalike. Meta finds people who resemble your real buyers — the closest it gets to "find more ML teams." 3 · Interest stack (cold prospecting). Layer interests: PyTorch, TensorFlow, Hugging Face, NVIDIA, CUDA, AWS, Google Cloud, MLOps, "artificial intelligence", Y Combinator, plus behaviors like "small business owners" / "tech early adopters". Keep it broad and let Meta's algorithm optimize — don't over-narrow.

Campaign setup

Structure
Objective: Leads or Sales (conversion) — never "Boost"/awareness. Optimize for a Quote Request event. Funnel: ad → landing page (the homepage savings calculator, or a dedicated LP) → quote form → lead lands in your dashboard. Budget: start ~$20–40/day for 7–10 days per audience; kill what doesn't produce a cost-per-lead you'd pay, scale what does. Creative: 3–4 variations, single image or 15s video. Let the price/pain self-select the audience. Measure: cost per quote request, then cost per closed customer — not clicks or impressions.
