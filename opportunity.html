Site strategy & energy

Power-ready space, wherever it already exists.

The site doesn't have to be a warehouse — it has to have power. We deploy on two archetypes, and we bring our own install crew so the electrical work is never a dependency on the landlord.

Archetype A · now

Leased commercial / light-industrial

Buildings that already carry heavy 3-phase service. Lease the space, install the node, go live in days. No new construction, no interconnection queue.

  • Power is already at the panel
  • Fewer noise / zoning frictions
  • Fastest path to the first owned nodes
Archetype B · expansion

Service-upgraded residential, self-generating

Our crew upgrades a home to 400A service — at which point it becomes a commercial-grade power customer — then deploys the node on-site, paired with max-fit rooftop solar + battery through a solar partner (e.g. Sunrun).

  • Solar + storage offsets a large share of energy cost and adds resilience
  • The homeowner gets the solar & backup; we get a power-ready site
  • A distribution channel competitors can't easily assemble

Honest framing on solar: a node runs 24/7 (~240 kWh/day); ~20 kW of rooftop solar yields on the order of 100 kWh/day, so on-site solar + battery is a meaningful energy-cost offset and resilience layer — not a full off-grid supply. The upgraded utility service remains the primary feed. The partner relationship handles solar/storage capital and install; we underwrite to grid power and treat self-generation as upside.

The precedent

The model is already proven — and already being funded.

Two real-world precedents de-risk the "distributed assets, coordinated by software" thesis. We're not inventing the architecture; we're the managed, demand-side layer that rides on top of it.

Precedent 01 · power

Tesla's Virtual Power Plant

Tesla aggregates tens of thousands of distributed home batteries and solar into a single, software-coordinated grid asset. In July 2025, California VPPs delivered >535 MW — with Powerwall homes alone providing ~500 MW.

Grid-scale capacity, assembled entirely from distributed hardware + AI coordination. Heliode applies the identical playbook to compute instead of power: a coordinated fleet of nodes behaving like one cloud.

Precedent 02 · compute

Span + NVIDIA's XFRA

In 2026, Span and NVIDIA launched XFRA — a distributed data-center network using existing electrical capacity to run GPUs in homes and small commercial space, targeting ~1 GW by 2027.

The validation is the point: NVIDIA is now funding the exact "go where the power already is" thesis. Heliode's wedge is a different layer — the managed service that signs and serves the end inference teams, sourcing today and owning capacity next, with our own service + self-generation site model.

When a $T-scale player puts capital behind your thesis, the question stops being "will this happen?" and becomes "who owns the customer and the deployment." That's the layer we're building.

Straight talk

The constraints we manage on purpose

We'd rather you know the weak spots. Here are the dependencies the whole model rests on.

DEPENDENCY 01

Offtake comes first

A node is a ~$250K brick without a contracted buyer. Compute revenue is the business. "Build it and they'll come" is the failure mode — the brokerage exists to prove and secure demand before we deploy. Power-starved hyperscalers and neoclouds are a natural anchor-offtake buyer for whole sites or regions, alongside the inference-team book.

DEPENDENCY 02

Hardware is financed, not bought

Equity funds the company, team, software, and a few proof nodes. Node hardware is financed against contracted compute revenue — equipment financing secured by signed offtake.

DEPENDENCY 03

Deployment is the moat — not price

Compute will commoditize; we plan for a 6%/yr price decline. What won't commoditize is securing power-ready space and standing compute up fast. The durable asset is the customer and site network.

DEPENDENCY 04

GPU supply favors large buyers

Top-class GPUs are supply-constrained. Sourcing and allocation must be solved early; a partner relationship can help, without becoming a single point of failure.
