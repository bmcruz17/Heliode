The problem

Compute is the bottleneck, and power is the binding constraint.

Inference is overtaking training as the dominant AI workload: steady, latency-sensitive, growing fast. Centralized supply can't keep up.

Demand

Inference, 24/7

Production AI runs continuously and close to users — a different load than bursty training.

Supply

5–7 year grid queues

New data centers are multi-year, multi-billion-dollar builds gated by interconnection and permitting.

The opening

Deployment nobody owns

Securing power-ready sites and standing compute up fast is hard work that remains wide open.

The solution

One managed layer between the team that needs compute and the metal that runs it.

Inference teams get one endpoint, one bill, uptime and support — at a price below their current cloud spend. We source on the open market today; we deliver from owned, self-powered nodes next.

  • Open-market sourcing now — marketplace + spot capacity well under hyperscaler on-demand, passed through with a managed wrapper.
  • We handle the babysitting — provisioning, monitoring, support, billing. The customer points at one endpoint, not a pile of accounts.
  • One contract, one invoice — predictable cost, no surprise egress math, no lock-in.
Why now

The architecture is already proven — and already being funded.

Distributed assets coordinated by software is a proven pattern. Heliode is the managed, demand-side layer that rides on top of it.

Precedent · power

Tesla's Virtual Power Plant

Tens of thousands of distributed home batteries behaving as one grid asset — California VPPs delivered >535 MW in July 2025. Grid-scale capacity assembled entirely from distributed hardware + coordination.

Precedent · compute

Span + NVIDIA's XFRA

In 2026, Span + NVIDIA launched a distributed data-center network running GPUs on existing electrical capacity in homes and small commercial space — targeting ~1 GW by 2027. NVIDIA is funding the exact "go where the power already is" thesis.

Our wedge is a different layer: they build distributed supply; Heliode is the managed demand layer that signs and serves the end inference teams — and can source from many supplies, including networks like theirs.
The model

Two phases: prove demand first, then own the network.

Phase 1 · now · asset-light

Managed brokerage

Source open-market capacity, wrap it in a managed service, resell to inference teams. Near-immediate revenue, near-zero CAC under a quote-first offer, and the demand proof that finances Phase 2.

Phase 2 · next

Owned regional capacity

Owned GPU nodes on power-ready sites, financed against signed offtake. We own the hardware and the multi-year customer relationship; the customer + site relationship is the durable asset.

The differentiator

Priority sites are service-upgraded homes that power themselves — and earn tax credits.

Our crew upgrades a home to 400A (a commercial-grade power customer), installs the node, and pairs it with a ~20+ kW solar array + battery. Solar offsets the node's biggest cost — power — and the battery adds resilience and grid revenue.

Why Heliode captures it

We own the energy asset

Because Heliode owns the solar + battery as a business asset (not the homeowner), the site claims the commercial §48E Investment Tax Credit — 30% base (auto-qualifies under 1 MW), with stackable +10% domestic-content and +10% energy-community adders — up to ~50%. The residential homeowner credit expired end of 2025; ownership is what unlocks value.

Stacked returns

~$38K–$53K back in year one

On a ~$80K system: the credit plus 100% first-year bonus depreciation returns roughly half to a third of the cost in year one. Battery storage keeps the full §48E through ~2033 and can earn VPP / demand-response revenue — long runway for our 2027+ rollout; the solar credit is upside we optimize, not a deadline we race.

Not tax advice — credit eligibility and the third-party-ownership structure to be confirmed with a CPA / tax attorney.
